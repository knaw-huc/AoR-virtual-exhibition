About this site

VE AoR, About this site

This website presents research of the project “The Art of Reasoning. Techniques of Scientific Argumentation in the Medieval Latin West (400-1400)” [https://en.huygens.knaw.nl/projecten/the-art-of-reasoning/], based at Huygens ING-KNAW (Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences). The project was financed by NWO, the Dutch Research Council. from June 2016 – June 2020. The team consisted of Irene O’Daly [https://nl.linkedin.com/in/irene-o-daly], Irene van Renswoude [https://www.huygens.knaw.nl/medewerkers/irene-van-renswoude/], and Mariken Teeuwen [https://www.huygens.knaw.nl/medewerkers/mariken-teeuwen/]. Practical and technical support was offered by Renée Schilling [https://www.huygens.knaw.nl/medewerkers/renee-schilling/]. Katia Riccardo, RMA student at Utrecht University (Medieval Studies) and intern for the project, helped create text portraits. The website was created by Bas Doppen [https://nl.linkedin.com/in/bas-doppen-b50a1931]. The software used for the site is ...

Colorbleed [https://www.colorbleed.nl/] created the animated exhibition trailer. The collaboration of us four manuscript geeks with the world of advertisement and animation was wildly inspiring: telling a story in just a hundred words and lining it up to powerful and beautiful visualisations really is an art! We thank Tom Hankins, Roy Nietrau, Daniel Visscher and Hert Zollner for their unrelenting effort.

The site shows images from library collections, mostly Leiden, University Library, and Paris, Bibliothèque Nationale de France. We are grateful for their explicit permission to show their images in this site. We also used images from other collections, for example the wonderful databases Initiale [http://initiale.irht.cnrs.fr/] or BVMM [https://bvmm.irht.cnrs.fr/]; e-codices, the Virtual Manuscript Library of Switzerland [https://www.e-codices.unifr.ch/en]; and the Parker Library on the Web [https://parker.stanford.edu/parker/], which have been made available for re-use for academic purposes under the terms of CC-BY licences. For collections that do not have an open permission to use their images (Karlsruhe, Badische Landesbibliothek; ... check!), we secured individual permissions for images. Please note that the publication of the images on this website does not grant permission to other parties to use them: permission has to be checked and secured by each party from each collection.

We aim at an audience of manuscript lovers. The goal is to take the visitor on a guided tour through the manuscript’s pages. With this we offer something that is fundamentally different from the static showcasing in glass boxes that museums may provide, or the unguided individual browsing through digitized collections one may do on one’s own computer.

We sincerely hope that you like our effort. Perhaps teachers may find use for the site in their teaching, or students may see the benefit of consulting (digitized) manuscripts instead of text editions. If you have any comments, please contact us! [mariken.teeuwen@huygens.knaw.nl]
