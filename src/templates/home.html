Explore four themes

Rhetoric and Dialectic

The arts of speaking (rhetoric) and reasoning (dialectic, or logic) were an essential part of the medieval curriculum.

Teachers and Students

Manuscripts carry evidence of how teachers and students taught and learned texts.

Glosses and Diagrams

Annotations guided a reader through texts, helping them understand and memorise works and concepts.

Debate and Controversy

Arguing was a crucial skill taught in the schools and used both in the classroom and in the world beyond.

Explore the manuscripts

In manuscripts we can see the traces of teachers, students, and readers: they left their markings, thoughts, schemes and drawings in them for us to explore. Read more about this here, where we explain how these portraits of manuscripts allow us to look over the shoulder of medieval writers and readers.
