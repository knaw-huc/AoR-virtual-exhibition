About this site

This website presents research of the project “The Art of Reasoning. Techniques of Scientific Argumentation in the Medieval Latin West (400-1400)” [https://en.huygens.knaw.nl/projecten/the-art-of-reasoning/], based at Huygens ING-KNAW (Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences). The project was financed by NWO, the Dutch Research Council. from June 2016 – June 2020. The team consisted of Irene O’Daly [https://nl.linkedin.com/in/irene-o-daly], Irene van Renswoude [https://www.huygens.knaw.nl/medewerkers/irene-van-renswoude/], and Mariken Teeuwen [https://www.huygens.knaw.nl/medewerkers/mariken-teeuwen/]. Invaluable practical and technical support was offered by Renée Schilling [https://www.huygens.knaw.nl/medewerkers/renee-schilling/]. Katia Riccardo, RMA student at Utrecht University (Medieval Studies) and intern for the project, helped create text portraits. Aim

We aim at an audience of manuscript lovers. The goal is to take the visitor on a guided tour through the manuscript’s pages. With this we offer something that is fundamentally different from the static showcasing in glass boxes that museums may provide, or the unguided individual browsing through digitized collections one may do on one’s own computer.

We sincerely hope that you like our effort. Perhaps teachers may find use for the site in their teaching, or students may see the benefit of consulting (digitized) manuscripts instead of text editions. If you have any comments, please contact us! [mariken.teeuwen@huygens.knaw.nl]
