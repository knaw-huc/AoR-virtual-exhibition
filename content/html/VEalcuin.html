Dialogue was a powerful tool in teaching, as Plato already showed in the dialogues of Socrates and his students: a student could ask his teacher questions, and thus learn from the master’s knowledge, or the teacher could ask his student questions, and thus check whether he had learned properly about the topics at hand.

±col2±

Alcuin (735−804), the great scholar at the court of Charlemagne (742-814), very much identified as a teacher in his works. He wrote his introduction to the Art of Reasoning as a dialogue between himself and Charlemagne, with Charlemagne asking questions and Alcuin answering.
